Current Non-Research Projects

KatsMD.com

I created a clinical website with two purposes:

  1. (KatsMD.com/info) to provide my clinic patients with educational resources
  2. (KatsMD.com/ref) to create a unified directory of clinical resources for myself (or other clinicians) to reference

Amoxicillin-clavulanate (Augmentin) Calculator

I, together with a Pediatric Pharmacist, created an online decision support tool to guide selection and dosing of the appropriate formulation of amoxicillin-clavulanate. The main question we answer is: "What ratio of amoxicillin to clavulanate should I use?"

Past Projects

Infusion Calculator

I, together with a Pediatric Pharmacist, created an online calculator to double-check nurses'/pharmacists' calculations for how to properly (according to the Massachusetts General Hospital protocol) up-titrate the infusions of IV immunoglobulin and rituximab.

Dr. DANce

Obesity is a huge problem in the U.S., and I wanted to do something about it. I created a website to make it as easy as possible for people to exercise (i.e. at home, without any equipment other than a phone/computer to access the website). I work on making everything as easy as possible, as well as explaining everything (how I made the website, why I made it, etc.). My vision is for this to be used by pediatricians and PCPs to encourage their patients to be active. Ideally, the website explains everything, so the physician won't need to spend much time on this; just a quick plug/"prescription" and the patient can look into it on their own time.

Sex Education Website

I am creating a sex education website that I could give to my patients to augment what we talk about during our clinic visits. The goal is not to create a comprehensive curriculum, but rather to create lists of high quality resources so that kids (and parents) know where to go for answers.

MGH Interpreter Service Phone Tree Navigator

I created a website to generate QR codes that would dial MGH Interpreter Services and would automatically navigate the phone tree to dial the patient's language, MRN, location in the hopsital, etc. The QR codes could be printed and posted at patients' bedsides to speed up the process of dialing for an interpreter to less than 30 seconds.
