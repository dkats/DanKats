Infantile spasms in children with Down syndrome

Sickle Cell Disease — Massachusetts General Hospital Comprehensive Sickle Cell Disease Treatment Center with Sharl Azar, MD

Sickle Cell Organized Outpatient Transition (SCOOT)

  • Currently, there is no intake process for the adult sickle cell clinic. We are developing an intake workflow so that new patients can be onboarded in an organized and holistic way.
  • Transition from pediatric clinic to adult clinic is currently unstandardized and happens over a span of 0–2 visits. We are creating a structured approach to transition patients. Rather than creating a strictly medical process, we are working with Psychiatry, Social Work, Child Life, Music Therapy, and Acupuncture.

Medical School

Lung Cancer Screening — MetroHealth System with Yasir Tarabichi, MD, MSCR

Inaccuracy of smoking history documentation in electronic health records: As we showed in CHEST (2018), smoking histories are wildly inaccurate in EHRs. Ideally, patients who were active smokers would have their duration of smoking increased over time (duh), and the amount they smoked ("pack-years") would increase as well (double duh). But that's not what we saw. Patients' smoking data did wild things, zig-zagging all over the place. Active smokers' durations of smoking would either stay constant over time or even decrease, as would their pack-years!

  • What does this mean for lung cancer screening?
  • What effect does this have on lung cancer prediction?

Provider habits with respect to ordering lung cancer screening?

  • Do providers have an intuitive sense of lung cancer risk?
  • What affects whether providers order lung cancer screening?
  • Do different variables affect ordering lung cancer screening vs. completion of the order?

Improving prediction of lung cancer: Since documentation of smoking history in EHRs is so inaccurate, what can we do to clean the data and make it more useful? We developed "history-conscious" metrics that attempt to be more representative of patients' true smoking exposures just by using data that is already in the EHR. So far, we have surprisingly found that these metrics do not make lung cancer risk calculators any more senstive or specific, but we will continue exploring ways to improve lung cancer predictions to better pick candidates for lung cancer screening.

Undergraduate

Inosine-5'-monophosphate Dehydrogenase — Lizbeth Hedstrom, PhD, Brandeis University

My undergraduate honors thesis (biochemistry) involved studying the previously undescribed interactions between inosine-5'-monophosphate dehydrogenase (IMPDH) and RNA polymerase and between IMPDH and ribosomes. IMPDH's main function is to convert inosine monophosphate to xanthine monophosphate as part of the synthesis of guanine nucleotides (the G building blocks of DNA and RNA). A previous member of the Hedstrom Lab had found that IMPDH binds to RNA polymerase and separately to ribsomes, even though IMPDH's catalytic function has nothing to do with either of them. While I was not able to reproduce the previous student's results, I developed a way of gene editing the E. coli that was much cleaner than the standard method. Usually when modifying genes, it's necessary to insert a "scar" sequence that theoretically doesn't do anything, but does add noncoding DNA. However, I was able to edit the DNA of my bacteria without any scar, making my cells as close to wild type as possible.
